Frequently Asked Questions

What are fire lane striping requirements?

Fire lanes must be clearly marked with red or yellow curb paint and pavement markings. Text should read "NO PARKING FIRE LANE" or similar. Width requirements vary by jurisdiction but typically range from 20-26 feet to accommodate fire apparatus.

How often should fire lane markings be refreshed?

Fire lane markings should be inspected annually and refreshed every 1-3 years depending on traffic volume and weather conditions. Faded or unclear markings can result in code violations and compromised emergency access.

What properties require fire lane striping?

Commercial buildings, apartment complexes, shopping centers, hospitals, schools, and industrial facilities typically require designated fire lanes. Requirements are determined by building size, occupancy, and local fire codes.

Can you mark fire hydrant zones?

Yes, we provide fire hydrant zone markings including the required clearance areas around hydrants (typically 3 feet on all sides and 15 feet in front). We can paint curbs red and add pavement markings as needed.

Do you coordinate with fire marshals?

Absolutely. We work directly with local fire marshals to ensure all markings meet current code requirements. We can provide site plans and documentation for your fire safety inspections and records.
