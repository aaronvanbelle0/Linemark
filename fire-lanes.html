Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our Fire Lane & Emergency Access striping services in Whatcom County

How often do fire lanes need to be repainted?

To remain compliant with local fire codes, markings must be clearly visible at all times. In the Pacific Northwest, rain and UV exposure can fade red curb paint over time. We generally recommend restriping every 1–2 years, or immediately if a Fire Marshal issues a warning, to ensure emergency zones are distinct and you avoid potential fines.

How long does the paint take to dry?

We know you can’t shut down your parking lot for long. We use fast-drying, industrial traffic paint that is typically dry to the touch in 30 minutes and ready for traffic in about an hour (weather permitting). We can also schedule work during low-traffic times to minimize disruption to your tenants or customers.

What areas do you serve for Fire Lane Striping?

We provide fire lane and curb painting services for commercial properties, retail centers, and HOAs throughout Whatcom County. This includes Lynden, Bellingham, Ferndale, Blaine, Everson, and Nooksack. We also serve clients in parts of Skagit, Snohomish, San Juan, and Island Counties.

How is the cost calculated for fire lanes?

Pricing is typically based on the linear footage of red curbing required plus a set price per stencil (e.g., "NO PARKING - FIRE LANE"). Compared to the cost of a municipal fine for non-compliance, restriping is a small investment. Contact us today for a free, on-site measurement and estimate.
