Parking Lot Striping Maintenance Schedule

Regular re-striping is essential for maintaining a safe, professional appearance and ensuring compliance with regulations.

Standard Traffic Areas

Every 2-3 years: Typical parking lots with moderate vehicle traffic

High Traffic Areas

Every 1-2 years: Shopping centers, apartment complexes, busy commercial properties

Pacific Northwest Climate

Annual inspection recommended: Wet climate can accelerate wear

ADA Compliance Markings

Maintain visibility: Federal law requires clear, visible accessibility markings

ADA Compliance Essentials

The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) sets specific requirements for accessible parking spaces and markings.

  • Accessible parking spaces must be at least 96 inches wide with an adjacent 60-inch access aisle
  • Van-accessible spaces require a 96-inch wide access aisle
  • International Symbol of Access must be displayed at each accessible space
  • Number of required spaces varies based on total parking lot size
  • Location requirements mandate accessible spaces be closest to facility entrance

Important: Non-compliance can result in fines up to $75,000 for first violations and $150,000 for subsequent violations. Contact LineMark Striping for a free ADA compliance assessment.

Parking Lot Maintenance Best Practices

Maximize your parking lot's lifespan and appearance with these professional recommendations.

Regular Inspections

Walk your property quarterly to identify fading lines, damaged markings, or safety concerns before they become violations.

Seasonal Timing

Schedule striping in spring or summer when temperatures exceed 50°F and weather is dry for optimal paint adhesion.

Surface Preparation

Clean and repair asphalt before striping. Fill cracks and potholes to ensure smooth, professional line application.

Quality Materials

Use high-grade traffic paint or thermoplastic for longevity. Cheap materials fade quickly and require more frequent repainting.

Documentation

Keep records of striping dates and specifications to track maintenance cycles and demonstrate due diligence for compliance.

Professional Service

Hire licensed, insured contractors with proper equipment. DIY striping often results in uneven lines and compliance issues.
