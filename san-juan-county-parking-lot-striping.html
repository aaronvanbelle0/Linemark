Frequently Asked Questions — San Juan County Parking Lot Striping

Common questions from Friday Harbor, Orcas Island, Lopez Island & San Juan County property managers

How often should parking lots in San Juan County be restriped?

Due to Northwest Washington's wet climate and salt air exposure unique to the San Juan Islands, we recommend a restriping schedule of every 2–3 years for most commercial properties. High-traffic ferry terminal areas near Friday Harbor and Orcas Island ferry landings may need annual restriping. Marina and waterfront properties often face accelerated paint wear from salt spray and should be inspected annually.

What are the ADA requirements for parking in San Juan County?

Washington State requires 1 accessible space for the first 25 parking spots, plus 1 additional space for every 50 spaces thereafter. All ADA spaces must be 8 feet wide with a 5-foot access aisle. We ensure full compliance with federal ADA guidelines and Washington State regulations — critical for tourist-heavy island communities like Friday Harbor's waterfront district and Eastsound village.

How do you get equipment to the San Juan Islands for striping projects?

LineMark coordinates all San Juan Island projects around Washington State Ferries schedules from Anacortes. We plan equipment transport on the WSF ferry routes to San Juan Island, Orcas Island, and Lopez Island, and schedule multi-site visits to maximize efficiency per island crossing. Ferry logistics are built into our quoting process — no surprise surcharges added after the fact.

Can you stripe parking lots in winter in San Juan County?

Striping requires dry pavement and temperatures above 50°F. In San Juan County, this typically means April through October. We recommend scheduling during drier summer months for optimal paint adhesion, and can coordinate around your peak tourist season to minimize disruption. Contact us to plan your project for the upcoming season.
