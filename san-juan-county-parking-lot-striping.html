Frequently Asked Questions — San Juan County Parking Lot Striping

How often should parking lots in San Juan County be restriped?

Due to Northwest Washington's wet climate and frequent freeze-thaw cycles, we recommend a restriping schedule of every 2-3 years for most commercial properties. High-traffic ferry terminal areas near the Friday Harbor WSF dock or Orcas Island ferry landing may need annual restriping due to heavy seasonal tourist volumes.

What are the ADA requirements for parking in San Juan County?

Washington State requires 1 accessible space for the first 25 parking spots. All ADA spaces must be 8 feet wide with a 5-foot access aisle. We ensure full compliance with WA State regulations, which is critical for tourist-heavy areas like Friday Harbor's waterfront district and the Orcas Island ferry landing commercial zone.

Can you stripe parking lots in winter?

Striping requires dry pavement and temperatures above 50°F. In San Juan County, this typically means April through October. We recommend checking our winter maintenance guide to prepare your lot for the off-season, particularly for businesses that scale down after peak summer ferry traffic.

How long does striping take to dry in San Juan County?

In typical island weather conditions, our fast-dry paint sets within 30-45 minutes. We can work during off-hours or section off areas to keep your business operational during the striping process, and we coordinate with ferry schedules to ensure equipment arrives on-island efficiently.

Do you serve commercial properties outside San Juan County?

Yes! We provide parking lot striping throughout San Juan County and also serve Whatcom, Skagit, Snohomish, and Island Counties.
