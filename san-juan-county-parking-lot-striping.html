Frequently Asked Questions - San Juan County Parking Lot Striping

How often should parking lots in San Juan County be restriped?

Due to Northwest Washington's wet climate and frequent freeze-thaw cycles, we recommend restriping every 2-3 years for most commercial properties in San Juan County. High-traffic areas may need annual restriping.

What are the ADA requirements for parking in San Juan County?

Washington State requires 1 accessible space for the first 25 parking spots, plus 1 additional space for every 50 spaces thereafter. All ADA spaces must be 8 feet wide with a 5-foot access aisle (or 11 feet for van-accessible spaces). We ensure full compliance with federal ADA guidelines and Washington State regulations.

Can you stripe parking lots in winter?

Striping requires dry pavement and temperatures above 50°F. In Snohomish County, this typically means April through October. We recommend scheduling your project during the drier summer months for optimal paint adhesion and durability.

How long does striping take to dry in San Juan County?

In typical San Juan County weather conditions, our fast-dry paint sets within 30-45 minutes. We can work during off-hours or section off areas to keep your business operational during the striping process.

Do you serve commercial properties outside San Juan County?

Yes! We provide parking lot striping throughout Snohomish County and also serve Whatcom, Skagit, Island, and King Counties.
