Managing Slope & Grade Issues in Historic Fairhaven & Downtown Bellingham

ADA Compliance Guide

Updated January 2026

Achieving ADA compliance in historic districts like Fairhaven and downtown Bellingham presents unique challenges, particularly when addressing the maximum allowable slope of 2% (1:48 ratio) for accessible routes of travel. The hilly terrain of Northwest Washington combined with aging infrastructure requires specialized knowledge of regrading techniques, cross-slope management, and pavement rehabilitation. Property managers and business owners must balance federal accessibility requirements with the preservation of historic character while ensuring safe, compliant pedestrian access. This guide provides practical strategies for managing slope and grade issues in these challenging environments, helping you avoid costly violations and create truly accessible parking facilities.
