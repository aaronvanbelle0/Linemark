Frequently Asked Questions — Skagit County Parking Lot Striping

How often should parking lots in Mount Vernon be restriped?

Due to Northwest Washington's wet climate and frequent freeze-thaw cycles, we recommend a restriping schedule of every 2-3 years for most commercial properties in Mount Vernon and Skagit County. High-traffic areas may need annual restriping.

What are the ADA requirements for parking in Skagit County?

Washington State requires 1 accessible space for the first 25 parking spots. All ADA spaces must be 8 feet wide with a 5-foot access aisle. We ensure full compliance with federal ADA guidelines and Washington State regulations.

Can you stripe parking lots in winter?

Striping requires dry pavement and temperatures above 50°F. In Mount Vernon, this typically means April through October. We recommend scheduling your project during the drier summer months for optimal paint adhesion and durability.

How long does striping take to dry in Mount Vernon?

In typical Skagit County weather conditions, our fast-dry paint sets within 30-45 minutes. We can work during off-hours or consider night striping options to keep your business operational.
