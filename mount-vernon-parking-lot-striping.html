Frequently Asked Questions — Skagit County Parking Lot Striping

Common questions from Mount Vernon, Burlington, Anacortes & Sedro-Woolley property managers

How often should parking lots in Mount Vernon be restriped?

Due to Northwest Washington's wet climate and frequent freeze-thaw cycles, we recommend a restriping schedule of every 2–3 years for most commercial properties in Mount Vernon and Skagit County. High-traffic retail centers along Riverside Drive may need annual restriping. Anacortes coastal properties may also need more frequent maintenance due to salt air exposure.

What are the ADA requirements for parking in Skagit County?

Washington State requires 1 accessible space for the first 25 parking spots, plus 1 additional space for every 50 spots thereafter. All ADA spaces must be 8 feet wide with a 5-foot access aisle. We ensure full compliance with federal ADA guidelines and Washington State regulations, including proper signage requirements.

Do you do warehouse and industrial floor marking in Skagit County?

Yes. LineMark provides OSHA-compliant warehouse floor marking including forklift aisle designations, safety zones, pedestrian walkways, staging areas, and loading dock markings for industrial facilities throughout Skagit County — including the Port of Skagit industrial area and Anacortes refinery-adjacent facilities. See our warehouse marking service page for more details.

Do you stripe sports courts in Skagit County?

Yes. LineMark stripes pickleball courts, tennis courts, and basketball courts for HOAs, city parks, and schools throughout Skagit County. We have completed court projects in Mount Vernon (Lincoln Park, Hillcrest Park), Burlington City Park, Washington Park in Anacortes, and Sedro-Woolley community parks.

Can you stripe parking lots in winter in Skagit County?

Striping requires dry pavement and temperatures above 50°F. In Skagit County, this typically means April through October. We recommend scheduling during drier summer months for optimal paint adhesion and durability. Contact us to plan your project for the upcoming season.
