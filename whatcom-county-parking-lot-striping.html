Frequently Asked Questions — Whatcom County Parking Lot Striping

Common questions from Bellingham, Ferndale, Lynden & Blaine property managers

How often should parking lots in Whatcom County be restriped?

Due to Northwest Washington's wet climate and frequent freeze-thaw cycles, we recommend a restriping schedule of every 2–3 years for most commercial properties in Bellingham and Whatcom County. High-traffic retail centers along Meridian Street may need annual restriping. Properties near the coast in Birch Bay and Blaine may also need more frequent maintenance due to salt air exposure.

What are the ADA requirements for parking in Whatcom County?

Washington State requires 1 accessible space for the first 25 parking spots, plus 1 additional space for every 50 spaces thereafter. All ADA spaces must be 8 feet wide with a 5-foot access aisle. We ensure full compliance with federal ADA guidelines and Washington State regulations, including proper signage requirements.

Do you do warehouse and industrial floor marking in Whatcom County?

Yes. LineMark provides OSHA-compliant warehouse floor marking including forklift aisle designations, safety zones, pedestrian walkways, staging areas, and loading dock markings for industrial facilities throughout Whatcom County — including Port of Bellingham industrial areas and Ferndale's manufacturing corridor.

Do you stripe sports courts in Whatcom County?

Yes. LineMark stripes pickleball courts, tennis courts, and basketball courts for HOAs, city parks, and schools throughout Whatcom County. We have completed court projects in Bellingham, Ferndale (Civic Center Park, Pioneer Trails), Lynden, Blaine, and Birch Bay Village HOA.

Can you stripe parking lots in winter in Whatcom County?

Striping requires dry pavement and temperatures above 50°F. In Whatcom County, this typically means April through October. We recommend checking our guide on winterizing your lot for off-season maintenance. Contact us to plan your project for the upcoming season.

How much does parking lot striping cost in Bellingham?

Pricing depends on lot size, number of stalls, layout complexity, and whether ADA markings, fire lane painting, or warehouse markings are needed. LineMark provides free on-site estimates for all Whatcom County properties with responses within 24 hours. Call (360) 739-9927 or email for a fast quote.

How long does parking lot striping take to dry in Whatcom County?

In typical Whatcom County weather conditions, our fast-dry commercial traffic paint is dry to the touch in 30 minutes and ready for vehicle traffic within 1 hour. We can work during off-hours or section off areas of your lot to keep your business fully operational during the striping process.

Do you serve commercial properties outside Bellingham in Whatcom County?

Yes. LineMark provides parking lot striping throughout all of Whatcom County including Ferndale, Blaine, Lynden, Birch Bay, Everson, Sumas, Nooksack, and Sudden Valley. We also serve Skagit, Snohomish, Island, and San Juan Counties.

Do you do fire lane striping in Whatcom County?

Yes. LineMark provides IFC-compliant fire lane striping throughout Whatcom County including red curb painting, NO PARKING FIRE LANE stencils, and hydrant zone marking for commercial properties in Bellingham, Ferndale, Lynden, and Blaine. We help properties pass fire marshal inspections and avoid compliance fines.

What is the difference between restriping and a new parking lot layout in Bellingham?

Restriping means painting fresh lines over an existing layout — same stall positions, same flow, just refreshed paint. A new layout means redesigning the stall configuration, traffic flow, or adding ADA spaces from scratch. LineMark handles both. A new layout project can increase your usable stall count and bring the lot up to current ADA and fire code standards.
