The smartest court investment any HOA, park, or school in Northwest Washington can make.

Multi-sport courts are the fastest-growing facility upgrade across Northwest Washington right now — and for good reason. Instead of maintaining separate dedicated surfaces for pickleball, tennis, and basketball, a single well-designed multi-sport slab can serve all three sports simultaneously at a fraction of the per-sport cost. HOAs in Bellingham, Lynden, and Ferndale, city parks in Mount Vernon and Anacortes, and school districts in Everett and Marysville are all doing this — converting underutilized single-sport slabs into full multi-sport facilities.

The key is professional color-coded line design. LineMark plans the full layout before any paint is applied — choosing line colors, resolving overlapping areas, and ensuring each sport is clearly readable on the finished surface. A poorly planned multi-sport court is confusing and frustrating. A professionally designed one is used constantly.

More Users, More Value

A single-sport court serves one group at a time. A multi-sport court serves pickleball players in the morning, basketball after school, and tennis on weekends — making your amenity budget work three times as hard.

No Extra Surface Needed

Multi-sport overlays work on any existing slab. You don't need to pour new concrete or resurface — if the surface is in reasonable condition, LineMark can add sports to it within a single day.

Cost-Effective Per Sport

Adding a second or third sport during the same striping visit costs a fraction of what a separate mobilization would. One site visit, one setup, multiple sports — the math is easy.

HOA & Parks ROI

Multi-sport courts are consistently among the top-rated amenities in HOA community surveys. They increase property desirability, satisfy more residents, and reduce requests for additional court construction.

Color-Coded Clarity

LineMark designs the color scheme before painting — choosing distinct colors for each sport to minimize confusion. Players adapt within minutes. Properly designed multi-sport courts are intuitive, not cluttered.

One Maintenance Schedule

Instead of restriping three courts on three different schedules, a multi-sport court gets done in one visit. Less coordination, less downtime, less cost over the life of the facility.

How Color-Coded Multi-Sport Courts Work

The design phase is what separates a great multi-sport court from a confusing one.

Before any paint goes down, LineMark plans the full color layout. Each sport gets its own line color — chosen for maximum contrast against both the court surface and the other sport lines. We review the design with you before we start so there are no surprises.

Players adapt to color-coded multi-sport courts within minutes. The visual separation is intuitive — you quickly learn "my sport plays on the green lines" and the other colors fade into background context. Properly designed courts are used more, not less, than single-sport alternatives.

White

Most common for primary sport — highest contrast on green or blue surfaces

Yellow

Excellent secondary sport color — visible on dark surfaces, distinct from white

Orange

Popular for basketball — high energy, highly visible, traditional court association

Blue

Clean contrast on gray or tan asphalt — works well as a third sport color
