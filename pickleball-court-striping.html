Professional Pickleball Court Striping — Northwest Washington

Regulation lines. Commercial-grade paint. Built for the Pacific Northwest.

Pickleball is the fastest-growing sport in the Pacific Northwest — from community parks in Bellingham and Lynden to recreation facilities on the San Juan Islands. LineMark Striping is the contractor HOAs, city parks departments, schools, and private facility owners trust to get pickleball court lines right — on time, on budget, and to official IFP specifications.

Whether you need a single court restriped in Everett or Marysville, a tennis overlay in Mount Vernon or Anacortes, or a multi-court build-out in Oak Harbor or Coupeville, LineMark delivers crisp, durable lines using Sherwin-Williams commercial paint formulated for the PNW.

Court Restriping

Restore faded pickleball court lines to regulation IFP specs. We prep, re-layout, and apply a 2-coat commercial paint system ready for play the same day.

Tennis Court Overlays

Convert any tennis court into a dual-use facility. Up to 4 regulation pickleball courts fit on a single tennis court in a contrasting color.

New Court Layouts

New construction ready for marking? LineMark coordinates with your contractor to deliver regulation line layouts and ADA-compliant court access paths.

Multi-Sport Court Marking

Need pickleball and basketball on the same surface? LineMark designs and stripes color-coded multi-sport overlays for parks, schools, and HOAs.

HOA & Parks Projects

Fully licensed, bonded, and insured. We work with HOA boards, city parks departments, and property managers. Certificates of insurance available on request.

Multi-Court Packages

Planning 3 or more courts? We offer volume pricing and have completed projects up to 6 courts. We work with HOA boards, parks departments, and contractors.

Official Pickleball Court Dimensions

Every LineMark court is striped to IFP (International Federation of Pickleball) specifications.

Whether you're adding courts in Ferndale or Blaine, building at a park in Burlington or Sedro-Woolley, or converting a slab in Everett or Lynnwood, LineMark verifies every measurement before the first line is painted.
