Professional Pickleball Court Striping — Northwest Washington

Regulation lines. Commercial-grade paint. Built for the Pacific Northwest.

Pickleball is the fastest-growing sport in the Pacific Northwest — from community parks in Bellingham and Lynden to recreation facilities on the San Juan Islands. LineMark Striping is the contractor HOAs, city parks departments, schools, and private facility owners trust to get pickleball court lines right — on time, on budget, and to official IFP specifications.

Whether you need a single court restriped at a community center in Everett or Marysville, a tennis-to-pickleball overlay in Mount Vernon or Anacortes, or a multi-court build-out for a new development in Oak Harbor or Coupeville, LineMark delivers crisp, durable lines using Sherwin-Williams commercial paint formulated for the PNW's freeze-thaw cycles and heavy rainfall.

Court Restriping

Restore faded or worn pickleball court lines to regulation IFP specifications. We prep the surface, re-layout exact dimensions, and apply a 2-coat commercial paint system ready for play the same day.

Tennis Court Overlays

Convert any existing tennis court into a dual-use facility. Up to 4 regulation pickleball courts fit on a single tennis court. We stripe pickleball lines in a contrasting color so both sports remain fully playable.

New Court Layouts

New construction or resurfaced courts ready for marking? LineMark coordinates directly with your contractor to deliver color surface coats, regulation line layouts, and ADA-compliant court access paths.

Multi-Sport Court Marking

Need pickleball and basketball on the same surface? LineMark designs and stripes color-coded multi-sport court overlays for parks, schools, and HOA facilities across Northwest Washington.

HOA & Parks Projects

We work directly with HOA boards, city parks departments, and property managers. Fully licensed, bonded, and insured with certificates of insurance available. We handle permitting coordination on request.

Multi-Court Packages

Planning 3 or more courts? LineMark has completed large-scale pickleball projects including a 6-court build in Island County. We offer competitive volume rates and can work around your facility's operating schedule.

Official Pickleball Court Dimensions

Every LineMark court is striped to IFP (International Federation of Pickleball) specifications.

Understanding pickleball court dimensions is the first step to planning your facility. Whether you're adding courts to an existing tennis surface in Ferndale or Blaine, building new courts at a park in Burlington or Sedro-Woolley, or converting a multipurpose slab in Everett or Lynnwood, LineMark verifies every measurement before the first line is painted.

Dimension Measurement Notes
Court Length (total) 44 feet Same as a doubles badminton court
Court Width 20 feet Singles and doubles use the same court width
Kitchen (NVZ) Depth 7 feet Non-volley zone on each side of the net
Net Height — Sides 36 inches At the sideline posts
Net Height — Center 34 inches Dips slightly at the center
Minimum Play Area (width) 30 feet Includes out-of-bounds buffer zone
Minimum Play Area (length) 60 feet Recommended for recreational and tournament play
Pickleball courts per tennis court Up to 4 Overlay striping in contrasting color
