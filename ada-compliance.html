Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our ADA compliance striping services in Northwest Washington

How many handicap parking spaces do I need?

The number of required accessible parking spaces depends on your total parking lot capacity. For example, lots with 1-25 spaces require 1 accessible space, 26-50 spaces require 2, and so on. At least one space must be van-accessible. We'll help determine the exact requirements for your property based on current ADA guidelines.

What are the dimensions for ADA compliant parking spaces?

Standard accessible parking spaces must be at least 8 feet wide with a 5-foot access aisle. Van-accessible spaces must be level (2% max slope) and at least 11 feet wide (or 8 feet with an 8-foot access aisle). Access aisles must connect to an accessible path of travel. All spaces must be level and properly marked with the international symbol of accessibility.

Do I need signage in addition to striping?

Yes! In addition to ground markings, accessible parking spaces must have vertical signs mounted at least 60 inches above the ground per Washington State RCW 46.19.050. Van-accessible spaces require additional "Van Accessible" signage. While we focus on striping, we can coordinate with signage contractors or provide referrals to ensure complete compliance.
