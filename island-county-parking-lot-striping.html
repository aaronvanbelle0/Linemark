Frequently Asked Questions — Island County Parking Lot Striping

Common questions from Oak Harbor, Coupeville, Langley & Camano Island property managers

How often should parking lots in Island County be restriped?

Due to salt air exposure and heavy rainfall unique to Whidbey Island and Camano Island's Puget Sound location, we recommend restriping every 2 years for most commercial properties. High-traffic areas along Oak Harbor's SR-20 corridor may need annual restriping. Properties near NAS Whidbey Island and along the Puget Sound shoreline face accelerated paint wear from coastal moisture and salt spray.

What are the ADA requirements for parking in Island County?

Washington State requires 1 accessible space for the first 25 parking spots, plus 1 additional space for every 50 spaces thereafter. All ADA spaces must be 8 feet wide with a 5-foot access aisle. We ensure full compliance with federal ADA guidelines and Washington State regulations. For businesses in Coupeville's historic district, we address slope and grade challenges to ensure proper accessibility for all visitors.

Do you stripe sports courts in Island County?

Yes — and Island County is one of our most active sports court markets. LineMark has completed 6-court pickleball court projects in Island County, plus tennis court and basketball court work at facilities in Oak Harbor, Coupeville, Langley, and Camano Island. All courts are marked to IFP, USTA, and regulation standards.

Do you do warehouse and industrial floor marking in Island County?

LineMark provides OSHA-compliant warehouse floor marking including forklift aisle designations, safety zones, pedestrian walkways, staging areas, and loading dock markings for industrial facilities throughout Island County — including commercial properties near NAS Whidbey Island and Oak Harbor's industrial areas.

Can you stripe parking lots in winter on Whidbey Island?

Striping requires dry pavement and temperatures above 50°F. In Island County, this typically means April through October. We recommend scheduling during drier months for optimal paint adhesion, particularly given Whidbey Island's high coastal moisture levels. We can also coordinate around your peak tourist season and ferry schedules to minimize disruption.

How long does parking lot striping take to dry in Island County?

In typical Island County weather conditions, our fast-dry commercial traffic paint is dry to the touch in 30 minutes and ready for vehicle traffic within 1 hour. We can work during off-hours or section off areas to keep your business fully operational during the striping process.

Do you do fire lane striping in Island County?

Yes. LineMark provides IFC-compliant fire lane striping throughout Island County including red curb painting, NO PARKING FIRE LANE stencils, and hydrant zone marking for commercial properties in Oak Harbor, Coupeville, Langley, Freeland, and Camano Island. What is the difference between restriping and a new parking lot layout in Oak Harbor?

Restriping means painting fresh lines over an existing layout — same stall positions, same flow, just refreshed paint. A new layout means redesigning the stall configuration, traffic flow, or adding ADA spaces from scratch. LineMark handles both for Island County properties.
