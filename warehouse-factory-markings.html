Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our Warehouse & Industrial Marking services in Whatcom County

How durable are the floor markings?

Longevity depends heavily on traffic volume. In high-traffic industrial environments, forklift tires and pallet dragging cause wear over time. However, we use industrial-grade traffic paints and epoxies specifically formulated to resist tire tracking, abrasion, and chemicals, ensuring your lines last significantly longer than standard hardware store paint.

How long until we can drive forklifts over the lines?

We prioritize minimizing downtime for your operations. Standard industrial traffic paint is typically dry to the touch in 30 minutes and ready for heavy forklift traffic in 1-2 hours. If we are applying specialized epoxy coatings, cure times may be longer. We also offer night and weekend scheduling to ensure zero disruption to your workflow.

What areas do you serve for Industrial Striping?

We provide warehouse and factory floor marking services throughout Whatcom County, including Lynden, Bellingham, Ferndale, Blaine, Everson, and Nooksack. We also serve industrial clients in parts of Skagit, Snohomish, San Juan, and Island Counties. Contact us to discuss your facility's location and specific needs.

How much does this service typically cost?

Pricing for industrial projects is typically calculated by linear footage and varies based on the type of material chosen (standard traffic paint vs. durable epoxy) and surface preparation requirements. We offer competitive rates for facilities in Lynden and Bellingham, ranging from small shop restriping to large distribution center layouts. Contact us for a free, on-site estimate tailored to your floor plan.
