Professional Asphalt Sealcoating Services

Northwest Washington's wet winters and freeze-thaw cycles destroy unprotected asphalt fast. Professional sealcoating is the single highest-ROI maintenance step for any parking lot — and paired with fresh line striping, it transforms your property in one day.

Pavement Protection

Sealcoating blocks UV oxidation, water infiltration, and fuel/oil spills — the three main causes of premature asphalt failure in the PNW. A sealed lot can outlast an unsealed one by 10–15 years.

Why Sealcoating Matters in the Pacific Northwest

Northwest Washington gets 35–45 inches of rain per year. Unprotected asphalt absorbs that water, which works into cracks, freezes, expands, and destroys the pavement base from the inside. Sealcoating is the waterproof barrier that stops this cycle.

How Often Should You Sealcoat a Parking Lot in Washington?

In the Pacific Northwest, we recommend sealcoating commercial parking lots every 3–5 years. High-traffic lots (grocery anchors, gas stations, fast food) may need sealing every 2–3 years. Lower-traffic office or HOA lots can often go 5 years. The key is applying before the pavement grays out and cracks begin to propagate.

How long does sealcoating last in the Pacific Northwest?

Professionally applied sealcoating typically lasts 3–5 years in Northwest Washington's wet climate. We use formulations designed for Whatcom County's rain and freeze-thaw cycles to maximize longevity.
